# AI Systems, Workflow Design, and Structured Problem Solving

This repository contains a collection of projects, experiments, and design explorations focused on understanding complex systems and making them easier to operate, improve, and evolve.

Across my professional and personal work, I have consistently been interested in a similar challenge:

> How do we make complex work easier to understand, coordinate, and execute?

Whether working on technical systems, operational processes, software architecture, or AI-assisted workflows, I tend to approach problems by identifying areas where structure, visibility, or process can be improved.

---

# How I Think About Problems

## Structure & Organization

Many systems become difficult to maintain because information, responsibilities, dependencies, and decisions become scattered or unclear.

I am interested in approaches that make systems easier to navigate, understand, and evolve.

Topics include:

* Explicit structure
* Dependency visibility
* Traceability
* Information architecture
* System decomposition

Related projects:

* SCIS
* ProgramBuilder
* Software architecture frameworks

---

## Decision Systems

Complex decisions often have hidden dependencies and downstream consequences.

I enjoy designing systems that help people make decisions more deliberately by exposing tradeoffs, constraints, and impacts.

Topics include:

* Decision frameworks
* Dependency mapping
* Change impact analysis
* Architecture planning
* Validation workflows

Related projects:

* System Design Decision Framework
* Change Impact Engine
* Architecture workflow concepts

---

## AI-Assisted Workflows

I use language models extensively as planning, design, documentation, and analysis tools.

My interest is less about replacing people and more about improving how humans and AI collaborate on complex work.

Topics include:

* Multi-step workflows
* Structured prompting
* Context management
* Multi-model collaboration
* Human-AI coordination

Related projects:

* Atlas
* ProgramBuilder
* Workflow orchestration concepts

---

## Knowledge & Context

Many difficult problems are ultimately information management problems.

I am interested in systems that improve how information is organized, retrieved, and maintained over time.

Topics include:

* Knowledge organization
* Context continuity
* Semantic retrieval
* Information relationships
* Long-term project memory

Related projects:

* Atlas
* Knowledge management concepts
* Semantic infrastructure explorations

---

## Operational Improvement

A recurring theme throughout my career has been observing how work is actually performed and identifying opportunities to improve effectiveness.

This often involves introducing structure, documentation, visibility, planning processes, or workflow improvements.

Examples include:

* Inventory visibility improvements
* Build planning support
* Onboarding and training support
* Documentation improvements
* Process standardization efforts

This area connects many of the ideas explored elsewhere in this repository to practical operational environments.
